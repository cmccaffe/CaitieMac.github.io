Caitie McCafferty

I am a PhD student at University of Texas at Austin. I conduct my research in the labs of Edward M. Marcotte and David W. Taylor.

I am interested in interdisciplinary approaches to scientific problems. I get my inspiration from image processing and mathematics and apply these methods to answer biological questions regarding molecular mechanisms.

About

I received my Bachelors of Science in Chemistry from the University of Maryland in 2015. My undergraduate research focused dispersant development for oil spill remediation and shape-changing hydrogels. After graduation, I spent two years as postbaccalaureate fellow at the National Institutes of Health (NIH). I worked at the National Eye Institute in the Protein Biochemistry and Molecular Modeling group with Dr. Yuri Sergeev. At the NIH I assembled the unfolding mutational screen, which predicted misfolding mutations based on protein stability and correlated these mutations with inherited eye disease. In the fall of 2017, I joined the University of Texas at Austin graduate program. Find more about what I do here in the research section!

When not in the lab (computational or ) I enjoy an active lifestyle with my partner (Paul) and dog (Sybil). Whether it be running, biking, or hiking, I am happiest when I am outdoors. I also find peace in indoor bouldering and painting (check out my artwork section!). I find my artistic inspiration from my research and the intersection of the natural and mechanical world.
